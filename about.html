About Me

Hello, welcome! I am currently a PhD candidate in the Mechanical Engineering Department of Carnegie Mellon University working with Professor Carmel Majidi. I am a Pittsburgh native growing up just north of the city. Working at SML during the summers while back from college, I eventually joined the lab as a PhD student after graduating undergrad from the University of Richmond in Spring 2020. My work focuses on developing soft materials for soft robotic applications along with wearable energy harvesting. In particular, I explore novel actuation methods for soft robots, along with focusing on developing high performance liquid metal based soft materials and device packaging solutions to create thermoelectric energy harvesters to be both stretchable and compliant for wearable applications. Recently, my work has focused on system level integration of these energy harvesters to tackle battery free wearable health monitoring systems, along with development of improved liquid metal based thermal interface materials. Over the last few years I have been able to collaborate on projects and work with great researchers at Meta Reality labs, Arieca Inc, and AFRL. I’m always happy to discuss research, so feel free to connect with me over email or LinkedIn to introduce yourself.
